Risk management The Board has an established structured approach to risk management that has been further embedded in the business to support the delivery of the strategic and business objectives.
The Risk Committee is accountable for the risk management system, the reporting of key risks and the associated mitigating actions.
Following an assessment of each business, risks are formally identified, prioritised and recorded in a corporate risk register which is reviewed and updated twice each year with risk mitigation plans identified for all significant risks.
Pensions The net pension deficit has reduced from 131.9 million to 121.8 million in line with the commitment expressed last year to make contributions of 11.5 million per annum for a minimum of three years in respect of the past service deficit of the main UK defined benefit scheme.
The other net finance charges arising on the difference between the interest cost on liabilities less the expected return on scheme assets was 1.8 million in line with previous forecasts.
In addition to the switch of the main UK scheme assets to 50% bonds 50% equities completed in April 2004, the Group has now also rebalanced the strategic asset allocation for the defined benefit schemes in the USA to 50% bonds 50% equities allocation.
International financial reporting standards IFRS For accounting periods starting on or after 1 January 2005 all European listed companies are required to use IFRS to prepare their consolidated financial statements.
For FKI this means that the 2005 Interim Report, the 2006 Annual Report and all subsequent reports including all comparative information will be prepared using IFRS.
The Group has continued its preparatory work to ensure full compliance with these requirements.
Prior to the announcement of the Groups Interim results in November 2005, it is the Groups intention to restate the 2005 results on an IFRS basis to allow the impact to be interpreted and adequately understood.
