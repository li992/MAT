McCarthy & Stone plc Corporate Governance The Group is committed to high standards of corporate governance.
The Board is accountable to the Groups shareholders for good corporate governance.
This statement describes how the principles of corporate governance are applied to the Group and the Groups compliance with the code provisions set out in Section 1 of the Combined Code, prepared by the Committee on Corporate Governance chaired by Sir Ronald Hampel.
Statement by the Directors on The Group has been in full compliance with the provisions set out in Section 1 of the Combined Code Compliance with the Code of throughout the year with the exception of the following: Best Practice The Non-Executive Directors have not been appointed for specified terms provision A6.1.
However, the Articles of Association specify that Directors must retire by rotation every three years, so each term of appointment is for a limited period only.
The re-appointment of Non-Executive Directors is not automatic.
So far as Executive Directors are concerned the Remuneration Committee recognises that some service contracts for long-serving Directors currently have termination periods in excess of the recommended one year maximum provision B1.7.
More recently, new Directors have been appointed on one-year notice period contracts.
The service contracts of all Executive Directors are periodically reviewed in line with best practice in industry.
The Chairman is a member of the Remuneration Committee provision B2.2.
The Board considers that the Committee has maintained the appropriate balance in the year by virtue of a majority of independent Non-Executive Directors.

Internal Controls The Board is responsible for ensuring that the Company maintains a system of internal controls, including suitable monitoring procedures and for reviewing its effectiveness.
The system of internal controls is designed to manage rather than eliminate business risks and as such can only provide reasonable, and not absolute, assurance against misstatement or loss.
Throughout the year, the Group has been, and will continue to be, fully committed to a risk management process and operates by setting business targets within appropriate timeframes.
There is an ongoing process for identifying, evaluating and managing the significant risks faced by the Group.
This process has been in place throughout the year and up to the date of the report and accounts, and includes: Business activities are governed by comprehensive guidance provided by policies and procedures, which are regularly reviewed by the Board.
Regional day-to-day control and corporate compliance is achieved by a thorough process of financial, legal and operational manual documentation for every project from planning through to completion.
This includes specific Executive Committee control and authorisation over key stages of land purchasing, construction and other key phases, as well as regular reporting and review of financial results and non-financial performance.
The development and implementation of computer information systems throughout the Group will further enhance the controlling and monitoring of projects from inception to completion.
An approved annual budget and three year rolling workflow plan is produced.
Each month, detailed monthly reports of trading results, balance sheets and cashflows are produced.
These are subject to regular review and any unexpected variances from budgets are followed up by management.
Key financial risks are controlled through the use of clearly laid down authorisation levels and proper segregation of accounting duties.
Business control processes are supplemented by documented annual control self-assessment reviews in each Region, as well as an ongoing process of financial and operational internal audits carried out by qualified staff.
Results of these are regularly reviewed by the Board.
The Board confirms it has reviewed the effectiveness of the system of internal controls for the period covered by the accounts and finds that the Group has been in full compliance with provision D2.1 of the Combined Code and the Turnbull Guidance.
