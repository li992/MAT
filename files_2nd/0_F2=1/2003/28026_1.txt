14 Perpetual Income and Growth Investment Trust plc Report of the Directors for the year ended 31 March 2003 Introduction The Directors have pleasure in presenting their report together with the audited financial statements of the Company for the year ended 31 March 2003.
The Company was incorporated and registered in England and Wales as a public limited company on 2 February 1996 under the Companies Act 1985, registered number 3156676.
Companys Business The Company is an investment company as defined in Section 266 of the Companies Act 1985 and operates as an investment trust in accordance with Section 842 of the Income and Corporation Taxes Act 1985.
The Inland Revenue has approved the Company as an investment trust in respect of the year ended 31 March 2002 and this status is expected to continue.
During the financial year under review the Board has pursued the investment policy as summarised on the inside front cover of this document.
No significant changes to this policy are currently envisaged.
A review of the business is detailed in the Chairmans Statement on page 5 and the Managers Report on pages 6 to 8.

Special Business at the Annual General Meeting Shareholders will find on page 33 the notice of the forthcoming Annual General Meeting of the Company to be held on 22 July 2003.
In addition to the ordinary business of the meeting, four resolutions are proposed as special business, all of which will be proposed as Special Resolutions.
Special Resolution 6 seeks the renewal of the Directors authority to allot up to an aggregate nominal amount of 899,547 representing 5% of the issued share capital new ordinary shares.
Special Resolution 7 seeks the renewal of the Directors authority to disapply pre-emption rights.
Special Resolution 8 seeks the renewal of the Directors authority to purchase up to 26,968,431 representing 14.99% of the issued share capital of its own shares.
Special Resolution 9 seeks to increase the maximum aggregate of Directors fees allowable under the Companys Articles of Association from 50,000 to 75,000.
It is not the Directors current intention to raise the rate of fees paid to each Director.
