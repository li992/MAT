Directors report The directors present their report together with the audited financial statements for the year ended 31 December 2003.
Principal activities and review of developments The principal activities of the group are property investment, trading and development and the ownership and management of operating businesses with strong property asset backing.
The majority of the groups assets, excluding those held by the Global Switch group of companies Global Switch, are located in the UK with the remainder in the USA.
Global Switch owns properties in the UK, Europe, South East Asia and Australia.
It was decided during the year to close the New York ofce and the disposal programme of the USA properties commenced in the second half of 2003.
It was announced on 26 January 2004 that an offer had been made by Duelguide plc to acquire the entire issued and to be issued share capital of the company and that the offer had been recommended for acceptance by those directors who were regarded as independent for the purposes of the City Code on Takeovers and Mergers.
The outcome of the offer is not known at the date of this report.
Current and future developments are reviewed in the chairmans statement on pages 2 to 9.
Results The group loss for the year after taxation and minority interests amounted to 44.6 million compared with a loss of 15.8 million for the previous year.
The retained loss for the year was 50.3 million compared with a loss of 30.3 million for the previous year.
The consolidated profit and loss account is set out on page 29 and the consolidated statement of total recognised gains and losses on page 32.
Dividends An interim dividend of 2p per share was paid to shareholders on 30 October 2003.
It is not proposed to pay a final dividend.
Net assets The consolidated net assets at the year end were 920.3 million compared with net assets of 1,023.1 million at 31 December 2002.
The basic net asset value per ordinary share has fallen during the year from 363.6p to 326.1p.
The consolidated balance sheet is set out on page 30.
A reconciliation of the movements in shareholdersfunds during the year is given on page 32.

Environmental, community and health and safety responsibilities The group continues to strengthen its policies towards community relations and the environment and two of its directors, RE Butler and WN Hugill, have specic responsibility for implementation.

Employees The group considers training and development to be fundamental to the efficient, effective and profitable operation of its activities as well as a core requirement for the personal development of its employees.
With this in mind, the group is committed to the achievement and maintenance of the Investors in People awards.
The group is also committed to the principle of equal opportunity and the employment of disabled applicants with suitable aptitudes and skills.
Payment of suppliers The group negotiates payment terms with each of its major suppliers and abides by these terms, subject to satisfactory performance by the supplier.
Amounts owed to other suppliers are usually settled on or before the end of the month following receipt of a valid invoice.
