Report of the directors Report of the directors The directors present their Annual Report and the audited financial statements of the Company and its subsidiaries for the year ended 28 February 2003.
Principal activities and business review The principal activity of the Group has remained that of housebuilding.
Further details of the Groups activities during the year under review and of its prospects are included in the Chairmans statement, the Chief Executives review, the Operational review and the Financial review.
Results and dividends The profit on ordinary activities for the year, after taxation, amounted to 56,903,000 2002: 49,608,000.
The directors recommend the payment of a final dividend of 8.25 pence per share which, together with the interim dividend paid in January 2003, will make a total dividend of 12.1 pence per share for the year ended 28 February 2003.
The total dividend cost is 13,607,000, leaving a retained profit for the year of 43,296,000.
The final dividend will be paid on 3 July 2003 to shareholders on the register at the close of business on 6 June 2003.
The shares will be quoted ex-dividend from 4 June 2003.

Annual General Meeting The Annual General Meeting will be held on 20 June 2003 at 12.00 mid-day at The Queens Hotel, The Promenade, Cheltenham, Gloucestershire GL50 1NN.
An explanation of the special business to be considered at the meeting is set out below.
Appointing auditors Resolution 7 Following the conversion of the auditors  to a Limited Liability Partnership LLP from 1 January 2003,  resigned on 30 January 2003 and the directors appointed its successor,  LLP, as auditors.
A resolution to reappoint  LLP as auditors to the Company will be proposed at the Annual General Meeting.
Authority to allot shares Resolution 8 The directors authority to allot securities in the capital of the Company expires at the conclusion of the forthcoming Annual General Meeting.
Accordingly, Resolution 8 is to renew that expiring authority and to give the directors general authority to allot, pursuant to section 80 of the Companies Act 1985, 35,714,229 ordinary shares representing the Companys unissued ordinary share capital as at 28 February 2003, which is less than one third of the Companys issued share capital.
This authority would, unless previously renewed by shareholders, remain in force up to the conclusion of the Annual General Meeting of the Company to be held in 2004.
The directors have no present intention of issuing any shares other than in respect of the Companys executive share option schemes.
Disapplication of pre-emption rights Resolution 9 The directors authority, pursuant to section 95 of the Companies Act 1985, to issue equity securities for cash as if section 89 of the Companies Act 1985 does not apply expires at the conclusion of the forthcoming Annual General Meeting.
The directors consider it appropriate for a further authority to be granted and to remain in force up to the conclusion of the Annual General Meeting to be held in 2004.
Accordingly, Resolution 9, which will be proposed as a Special Resolution, is to give the directors authority to issue ordinary shares for cash to holders of ordinary shares on a pre-emptive basis subject to special arrangements relating to fractional entitlements or practical problems.
In addition, the directors will be authorised to issue ordinary shares for cash otherwise than on a pre-emptive basis up to an aggregate nominal value of 568,929, representing approximately 5% of the Companys issued ordinary share capital as at 28 February 2003.
Extraordinary General Meeting An Extraordinary General Meeting will be held on 20 June 2003 following the completion of the Annual General Meeting.
The matters to be considered at this meeting will be covered in a separate letter from the Chairman to shareholders.
