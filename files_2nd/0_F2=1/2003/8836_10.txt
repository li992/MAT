Acquisitions and Developments In April 2003 the Group acquired the assets of Barber Industries, a manufacturer and supplier of wellhead equipment and valve safety systems to the Canadian oil & gas production and pipelines industries.
In May 2003 the Group acquired Dutch engineering company Korndorffer Contracting International KCI.
KCI specializes in design engineering for oil & gas clients in the Netherlands.
With effect from December 2003, the Group ceased to account for ASCO plc as an associate as it effectively no longer exercises significant influence.
The Group's remaining investment in ASCO has been written off in full.
On 1 January 2004, the Group acquired Z. TEC GmbH Energy Service, a company based in Moers, Germany, that provides services to heavy industrial gas turbine users in Germany and other parts of Central Europe.
Further details of the impact of these acquisitions and developments are set out in notes 12 and 24 of the financial statements.

Creditor Payment Policy The Groups current policy concerning payment of its trade creditors by UK subsidiaries is to: a settle the terms of payment with those suppliers when agreeing the terms of each transaction.
b ensure that those suppliers are made aware of the terms of payment by inclusion of the relevant terms in contracts.
c abide by the terms of payment.
The payment policy applies to payments to creditors for revenue and capital supplies of goods and services.
UK subsidiaries follow this policy and overseas subsidiaries are encouraged to adopt similar policies by applying local best practices.
