Information on HICL Infrastructure Company Limited HICL Infrastructure Company Limited HICL or the Company or, together with its 100%-owned subsidiaries, the Group was the first investment company listed on the London Stock Exchange set up to invest in infrastructure projects.
It was launched in March 2006 as HSBC Infrastructure Company Limited, and raised 250m with which it purchased an initial portfolio of interests in 15 PFI PPP projects.
The Company changed its name to HICL Infrastructure Company Limited in 2011.
In May 2008, December 2009, December 2010, March 2012 and March 2013 the Company raised further equity capital by the issue of C Shares which subsequently converted into Ordinary Shares or New Ordinary Shares.
As at 21 May 2013, it has a portfolio of 83 investments in infrastructure projects in the UK, Canada, Holland and Ireland.

At the time of the first C Share issue in May 2008 the target period was revised to 7 years 2013 and the Company achieved this objective in the year to 31 March 2013.
The long-term target Internal Rate of Return IRR is approximately 7% as stated in the February 2013 New Ordinary Shares prospectus and based on a purchase price of 119.5p per share.
At launch in March 2006, 250,000,000 Ordinary Shares were issued.
With the conversion of C Shares and the issuance of New Ordinary Shares, together with Ordinary Shares issued as a result of tap issues and scrip dividends, the total Ordinary Shares in issue at 21 May 2013 was 1,120,252,107.
The Investment Adviser to the Company is InfraRed Capital Partners Limited, which is authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority.
Total headcount of the InfraRed group is over 100 and the infrastructure team now comprises 43 staff in offices in London, Paris, New York and Sydney.
Twelve are dedicated to advising the Group and a further five allocate a large part of their time to supporting the Group and its investments.
