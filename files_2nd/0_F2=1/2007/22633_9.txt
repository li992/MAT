Report of the Directors The directors present their report, together with the audited financial statements of the Company and its subsidiaries for the year ended 31 December 2007. Business Review Principal Activities Collins Stewart plc is a holding company directly owning the entire issued share capital of Collins Stewart Europe Limited and Hawkpoint Holdings Limited, the two principal direct operating subsidiaries of the Company.
The Group comprises the Company and all its subsidiary undertakings.
Details of the principal subsidiary undertakings are shown in note 36 to the consolidated financial statements.
The Group is an independent investment bank.
Founded in 1991, it has grown steadily and now has over 700 staff and a presence in 12 jurisdictions.
The Groups activities include institutional and private-client stockbroking, market making, corporate advisory services, fund management TM and the supply of on-line financial information through the quantitative research system Quest.
A review of the development and performance of the Group during the year is presented in the Chairmans Statement and the Operating and Financial Review on pages 10 to 23.
Results and Dividends The profit attributable to ordinary shareholders of the Company for the year ended 31 December 2007 amounted to 56.3m, compared with 41.5m for the year ended 31 December 2006, as set out in the Consolidated Income Statement on page 51.
An interim dividend of 2.5p 2006: nil per ordinary share was paid on 6 December 2007.
The directors recommend that, subject to approval at the Annual General Meeting, a final dividend of 5.0p 2006: 5.0p per ordinary share be paid on 29 May 2008 to members on the register on 9 May 2008.
This will make a total dividend for the year of 7.5p 2006: 5.0p per ordinary share.

Business Developments On 16 July 2007 Collins Stewart completed the acquisition of C. E. Unterberg Towbin Holdings Inc. CEUT the holding company of the US investment bank C. E. Unterberg Towbin.
The initial consideration of US $15.9m for the equity of CEUT as well as US $2.0m in certain employee payments was paid in cash on completion.
Deferred consideration is in the form of 2,755,511 Collins Stewart shares with a value of $12.1m.
The total acquisition cost was $32.6m inclusive of transaction costs.

Staff The Group employed an average of 693 employees each month during the year, and employed 707 at 31 December 2007.
The increase in the year was due primarily to the acquisition of CEUT.
Details of employee-related costs are set out in note 6 to the consolidated financial statements.
It is the Groups policy to give appropriate consideration to applications for employment from all persons, having regard to their particular aptitudes.
For the purposes of training, career development and promotion, the Group is committed to fairness and its policy is not to discriminate against any persons with regard to gender, age, disability, sexual orientation, religious or political beliefs but to develop and promote based on individual ability and the needs of the business.
The Group has a policy of keeping employees informed about major developments in the business.
In particular, announcements are made available to employees when released to the public and other communication channels such as the Corporate intranet are used to encourage employee engagement.
The ownership of shares in the Company is encouraged by the Board, who consider share ownership as an important means of providing an incentive to employees and aligning their interests with other shareholders.
In line with this, the Company launched a Share Incentive Plan SIP shortly after the year end which offers employees the opportunity to acquire shares in the Company on potentially tax advantageous terms.
Health and Safety Policy The health, safety, wellbeing and security of employees is a priority for the Group.
Regular reviews are undertaken of both policies and processes to ensure compliance with current legislation and best practice.
The Group focus is on ensuring that these policies are closely linked to the operational needs of the business.
28 Collins Stewart plc Annual Report & Accounts 2007 Social, Environmental and Ethical Matters The Board has adopted policies with regard to the social, environmental and ethical matters which affect the business.
These govern, inter alia, the type of business which is transacted, the way in which business is conducted and the approach to training and incentivising staff.
In particular: the Board takes regular account of social and ethical matters affecting the business: environmental issues are not considered on a regular basis as these are not regarded as a high risk: the Board periodically carries out formal assessments of the significant risks to its business which take account of social and ethical matters affecting both short and long term value.
The opportunities to create value arising from certain social and ethical stances are taken into account when formulating policies: the Board considers that it receives adequate information to make assessments of the social, environmental and ethical matters which affect its business: and there are systems of risk management in place to manage the significant risks which could affect the business.
The nature of the Groups activities is such that it has a minimal direct effect on the environment.
However, the Board has agreed that it will seek to adopt policies to safeguard the environment to meet statutory requirements or where such policies are commercially sensible.
Currently, where possible, waste paper is recycled and energy saving practices are employed.
