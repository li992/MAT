Electronic Communication The Companies Act 2006 has introduced provisions making it easier for companies to use the internet to communicate with shareholders.
Resolution 8 will give the Company power to use website communication for documents such as the Annual Report and Accounts.
If Resolution 8 is passed, the Company will send shareholders a written request to agree to website delivery and may deem a shareholder to have agreed unless the shareholder responds within 28 days.
If a shareholder declines website delivery, the Company is not permitted to send that shareholder another invitation to accept website delivery for the following 12 months.
Those who have not responded in time will still have the right to require the Company to send a hard copy of any information communicated electronically.
When using website delivery, the Company will notify each shareholder by post that information is available on its website, and how to access it.
The Company is proposing to adopt this procedure to save printing and distribution costs, as well as for environmental reasons.
