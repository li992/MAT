Alfred McAlpine plc 28 Annual Report and Accounts 2005 REPORT OF THE DIRECTORS The directors present their annual report and the audited accounts for the year ended 31 December 2005, which have been prepared in accordance with IFRS.
Principal activities The Group provides support services, principally in the UK, offering clients in the public, private and regulated sectors integrated solutions for all their built environment needs.
In recognition of the transformation of the business since 2001, the Company was re-classified on 19 September 2005, and is now listed within the Support Services sector of the FTSE indices.
The operations of the Group are reviewed on pages 12 to 25 of this report.
Profits and dividends The Group profit for the year attributable to equity shareholders amounted to 35.8m 2004: 11.1m after tax and one-off items.
After payment during the year of ordinary dividends amounting to 12.4m, the balance of 23.4m has been transferred to reserves 2004: 0.5m loss charged to reserves after payment of ordinary dividends amounting to 11.2m and preference dividends amounting to 0.4m.
The prior year figures have been re-stated following conversion to IFRS, a further effect of which is that preference dividends are now treated as a finance cost.
The directors recommend the payment of a final dividend of 7.85p per ordinary share which, together with the interim dividend of 5.45p already paid, makes a total of 13.3p per ordinary share for the financial year.
If approved at the Annual General Meeting AGM to be held on 6 June 2006, the final dividend will be paid on 16 June 2006 to those shareholders on the register at close of business on 26 May 2006.

Alfred McAlpine plc Annual Report and Accounts 2005 29 Employees It is the Groups policy to communicate with and involve employees on matters affecting their interests at work, and to inform them of the performance of the business.
In addition to an annual employee survey and a regular employee forum in each business stream, this is achieved by publishing a range of internal magazines and newsletters across the Group and holding regular team briefings and toolbox talks.
It is also Group policy to treat all employees and potential employees equally and to give full consideration to suitable applications for employment from disabled persons, where they have the necessary abilities and skills for the position, and wherever necessary and possible to retrain employees who become disabled, so that they can continue their employment in another position.
The Company operates an all-employee savings related share option scheme, the principal aim of which is to encourage eligible employees to purchase shares in the Company.
At 31 December 2005 there were 1,117 2004: 1,495 employees participating in this scheme, details of which are set out in note 21 on page 70.
During the course of 2006, the Alfred McAlpine plc Partnership Share Scheme 2000, an all-employee share incentive plan approved by shareholders in 2000, will also be brought into operation.
Donations Charitable donations of 74,053 2004: 38,856 were made during the year to UK registered charities, including 22,650 to Crisis at Christmas.
Other charities supported included Water Aid and various appeals relating to the Asian tsunami.
No political donations were made.
Share capital Since the last AGM, no offer has been made or agreement entered into which would require shares to be issued, other than in connection with the Executive Share Option Schemes, the Savings Related Share Option Scheme, the Restricted Share Plan and the Long Term Incentive Plan 2005.
On 12 August 2005, 300,000 ordinary shares of 25p each were allotted as part of the settlement of entitlements under the Stiell Networks Deferred Bonus Plan.
At 31 December 2005, the trustee of the Alfred McAlpine Employee Benefit Trust held 1,608,444 ordinary shares, being approximately 1.6% of the Companys issued ordinary share capital.
At 31 December 2005, the authority granted at the AGM held on 9 June 2005 to enable the Company to purchase up to 10,023,560 ordinary shares, representing 10% of its then issued ordinary share capital, was still valid.
The Company has not made use of this authority during the year.
The authority expires at the conclusion of the AGM to be held on 6 June 2006, and a resolution to renew the authority in full will be put to shareholders at that meeting.
Auditors  LLP have confirmed their willingness to continue in office, and a resolution concerning their re-appointment and remuneration will be proposed at the AGM.
Supplier payment policy It is the policy of both the Company and the Group to make payment on their standard terms to suppliers unless alternative terms are agreed.
The Company and the Group both seek to abide by these payment terms, provided that they are satisfied that the supplier has complied with the agreed terms and conditions.
