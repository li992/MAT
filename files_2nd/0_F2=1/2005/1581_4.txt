Financial Review for the year ended 31 December 2005 Profit before tax and acquisition restructuring costs totalled $18.4 million in 2005, up 142% from $7.7 million in 2004.
The increase in profit before tax and post-acquisition restructuring costs reflects an increase in revenues from 2004 to 2005 offset by increases in sales and marketing and research and development.
Profit before tax totalled $12.6 million in 2005, up 66% from $7.7 million in 2004.
Post-acquisition restructuring costs represent non-recurring expenses associated with acquisitions completed during 2005.
Income tax charges totalled $3.7 million in 2005, up 133% from $1.6 million in 2004.
Effective income tax rates increased from an effective group tax rate of 20.5% in 2004 to an effective rate of 29.1% in 2005, primarily due to increased profits in the United States, which has a higher corporate tax rate than the U. K. coupled with the fewer operating losses to offset against increased profits.
Net profit before acquisition restructuring costs totalled $12.4 million in 2005, up 103% from $6.1 million in 2004.
Net profit totalled $9.0 million, up 47% from $6.1 million in 2004.
Liquidity and Capital Resources Cash and cash equivalents totalled $68.6 million at 31 December 2005.
During 2005 cash was primarily utilized for the acquisition of etalk and Verity, Inc. together with the companys share repurchase program which was also conducted in 2004 as discussed below.
Net cash provided by operating activities totalled $16.9 million in 2005, an increase of 213% from $5.4 million in 2004.
The increase in cash generation from 2004 to 2005 was primarily related to increased revenues and strong cash collection during 2005.
Net cash used in generated by investing activities totalled $352.9 million in 2005, up from $ 1.2 million in 2004.
In 2005, net cash of $28.1 million was used for the acquisition of etalk, and net cash of $320.5 million was used for the acquisition of Verity.
Cash was also used during both of these periods to acquire office equipment and software licenses, and to invest in the joint venture.
Net cash provided by used in financing activities totalled $307.5 million in 2005, up from $ 8.7 million in 2004.
Net cash provided by financing activities in 2005 was primarily from the proceeds of the companys rights issue in November 2005 and the drawdown of the companys bank loan in December 2005, offset by cash used for the companys share repurchase program, which commenced during 2002 and continued into 2005.
During 2005, the company purchased for cancellation 593,942 shares 2004: 12,332,084 at an average price of approximately 1.97 per share 2004: 1.84.
The company has purchased for cancellation an aggregate of 20,997,824 shares at an average price of approximately 1.65 per share.
At 31 December 2005 the company had drawn down $48.9 million against the companys bank loan, as partial consideration for the purchase of Verity, Inc.
The companys commitment for 2006 under operating leases is $7.2 million, as discussed below.
The company does not have any material financial guarantees or related covenants.
Substantially all of the companys cash balances are held in short-term deposits paying market interest rates.
The company holds material cash balances in pounds sterling, U. S. dollars and Euros.
The company does not currently undertake currency hedging transactions to cover the companys transaction or translation exposures, but the company may choose to hedge a portion of these exposures in the future.
The directors believe that the companys current cash and cash equivalents and cash generated from operations will be sufficient to meet the companys anticipated cash requirements for working capital and capital expenditures for at least the next 12 months.
