38 VT Group Annual report and accounts 2009 Board of Directors Michael Jeffries Chairman, age 64.
Chairman of Nomination Committee and member of the Remuneration Committee.
Appointed as a non-executive director in May 2005, Michael became Chairman on 5 July 2005.
Michael was previously Chief Executive Officer of WS Atkins plc from 1995 to 2001 and Chairman from 2001 to 2005.
He is currently Chairman of NCP Services Limited and Wyless plc.
Paul Lester Chief Executive, age 59.
Became Chief Executive of VT Group in July 2002 having been a non-executive Board member since 1998.
Previously, Paul was a group managing director of Balfour Beatty plc.
A graduate mechanical engineer, he has held a number of Board or senior management positions in engineering and support services companies in both the UK and US.
He is currently a non-executive director of Chloride Group Plc and the President of the Society of Maritime Industries.
Chris Cundy Corporate Development Director, age 48.
Joined the Group from KPMG in 1993.
A chartered accountant, he was appointed to the position of Finance Director in 1997 and subsequently became a Board member in 1999.
Chris was appointed Commercial Director in January 2006 and to his current role of Corporate Development Director in April 2009.
He has no directorships outside the Group.
He is a trustee of Action for ME.
Philip Harrison Finance Director, age 48.
Joined the Board in November 2007 from Hewlett Packard where he was Vice President Finance, Europe, Middle East and Africa.
A fellow of the Institute of Chartered Management Accountants, he was previously with Compaq and Rank Xerox.
Peter McIntosh Executive Director, age 50.
Joined VT Group in 1992, becoming Managing Director of Fleet Support Limited in 1999.
Peter was appointed Managing Director of VT Shipbuilding and joined the Board in September 2003.
He resigned from the Board to take up a two-year secondment with the Ministry of Defence as Chief Executive of the Future Aircraft Carrier project, and returned to the Board in January 2008.
His current role includes responsibility for the Groups interests in BVT Surface Fleet and for developing VTs environmental services activities.
Peter will leave the Board and the Group following the exit from BVT later this year.
VT Group Annual report and accounts 2009 39 Andrew Given Non-executive Director, age 61.
Chairman of Audit Committee and member of Remuneration and Nomination Committees.
Appointed to the Board in September 2002.
Andrew retired as Deputy Chief Executive of Logica plc in December 2002 having previously been its Finance Director.
Prior to that he held roles in the telecommunications industry.
He is a non-executive director and chairman of the audit committee of Morgan Crucible plc.
David Barclay Non-executive Director, age 55.
Chairman of Remuneration Committee and member of Nomination and Audit Committees.
Appointed to the Board in May 2003.
With a background in the Civil Service and the City, David was a vice-chairman of Dresdner Kleinwort Wasserstein until March 2005.
He is non-executive deputy chairman of John Lewis Partnership plc and a non-executive director of Wessex Water Services Limited.
Baroness Blackstone Non-executive Director, age 66.
Member of Remuneration and Nomination Committees.
Appointed to the Board in January 2004.
After an academic career culminating in her appointment as Master of Birkbeck College, London, Baroness Blackstone served in the Government successively as Minister of State for Education and Employment 1997-2001 and Minister of State for the Arts 2001-2003.
She has now retired from active politics and has returned to the higher education sector as Vice Chancellor of the University of Greenwich.
She chairs the RIBA Trust and the Great Ormond Street Hospital for Children NHS Trust.
Admiral the Lord Michael Boyce Non-executive Director, age 65.
Appointed to the Board in July 2004.
After a long and distinguished career in the Royal Navy, culminating in his appointment as First Sea Lord in 1998, Lord Boyce was subsequently appointed Chief of Defence Staff in 2001.
He retired from the latter role in May 2003 and was appointed Lord Warden and Admiral of the Cinque Ports in July 2004.
He is a non-executive director of WS Atkins plc: Chairman of the RNLI and the White Ensign Association: an Elder Brother of Trinity House: and a Trustee of the National Maritime Museum.
Ian Tyler Non-executive Director, age 48.
Appointed to the Board in May 2008.
A chartered accountant, Ian has been Chief Executive of Balfour Beatty plc since 2005, having been Chief Operating Officer since 2002 and, prior to that, Finance Director.
He is currently president of Construction Industry Relief, Assistance and Support for the Homeless Ltd, the construction and property industry charity for the homeless.
