Acquisitions and Developments On 5 June 2002, the Company was listed on the London Stock Exchange.
As part of the initial public offering, each of the 1 Companys ordinary shares of 10 pence were subdivided into 3 ordinary shares of 3 3 pence each.
A total of 75,951,635 ordinary shares were issued by the Company at a price of 1.95 per share.
In early June 2002, the Group acquired a further 6.7% shareholding in Mustang Engineering Holdings Inc. for a consideration of $16.1m taking the Group shareholding in Mustang to 86.7%.
On 30 June 2002, the Group acquired Operators and Consulting Services, Inc. OCS which provides operations and maintenance services to oil and gas operators in the Gulf of Mexico.
In August 2002, the Group acquired Industrial Repair Services, Inc, a generator repair company based in New Mexico.
Also in August, the Group acquired 51% of Santos Barbosa Tecnica Comercio e Servicos Ltda, a Macae based provider of offshore modifications and maintenance services to the Campos Basin in the Brazilian offshore market.
In October 2002 the Group acquired the business and assets of California based Thomason Mechanical Corporation and sister company Bender Machine for a cash consideration $45.7m.
Thomason Mechanical is a major provider of rotating and reciprocating equipment field services to the North American power and petrochemical industries.
Bender Machine supplies precision machining and coating services to the energy industry.
In March 2002, the Group closed its aero engine overhaul business in Connecticut.
Further details of the impact of these acquisitions is set out in notes 2 and 24.

John Wood Group PLC 27 Report of the Directors Creditor Payment Policy The Groups current policy concerning the payment of its trade creditors is to: a settle the terms of payment with those suppliers when agreeing the terms of each transaction, b ensure that those suppliers are made aware of the terms of payment by inclusion of the relevant terms in contracts, c abide by the terms of payment.
The payment policy applies to payments to creditors for revenue and capital supplies of goods and services.
UK subsidiaries follow this policy and overseas subsidiaries are encouraged to adopt similar policies by applying local best practices.
