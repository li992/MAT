Directors report The directors present their report together with the audited financial statements for the year ended 31 December 2002.
Principal activities and review of developments The principal activities of the group are property investment, trading and development and the ownership and management of operating businesses with strong property asset backing.
The majority of the groups assets, excluding those held by the Global Switch group of companies Global Switch, are located in the UK with the remainder in the USA.
Global Switch owns properties in the UK, Europe, South East Asia and Australia.
Current and future developments are reviewed in the chairmans statement on pages 2 to 13.
Results The group loss for the year after taxation and minority interests amounted to 15.8 million compared with a loss of 44.1 million for the previous year.
The retained loss for the year was 30.3 million compared with a loss of 55.9 million for the previous year.
The group profit and loss account is set out on page 33.
Dividends The directors recommend a final dividend of 2.8p per ordinary share which, when added to the interim dividend, makes a total of 4.8p for the year.
This compares with a total dividend for the previous year of 4.8p.
Subject to approval by the members at the Annual General Meeting, the final dividend in respect of the year ended 31 December 2002 will be paid on 19 May 2003 to members on the register at the close of business on 28 March 2003.
Scrip dividend alternative Shareholders are being offered the opportunity to receive the final dividend in the form of new fully paid ordinary shares instead of cash.
Details of the offer are set out in the accompanying explanatory document.
Subject to approval by the members at the Annual General Meeting, new share certicates will be despatched for receipt by 19 May 2003 and CREST members will have their accounts credited directly with new shares on that date.
Net assets The group net assets at the year end were 1,023.1 million compared with net assets of 966.5 million at 31 December 2001.
The basic net asset value per ordinary share has fallen during the year from 394.7p to 363.6p.
The group balance sheet is set out on page 34.

Employees The group considers training and development to be fundamental to the efficient, effective and profitable operation of its activities as well as a core requirement for the personal development of its employees.
With this in mind, the group is committed to the achievement and maintenance of the Investors in People awards.
The group is also committed to the principle of equal opportunity and the employment of disabled applicants with suitable aptitudes and skills.
Payment of suppliers The group negotiates payment terms with each of its major suppliers and abides by these terms, subject to satisfactory performance by the supplier.
Amounts owed to other suppliers are usually settled on or before the end of the month following receipt of a valid invoice.
Group trade creditors at 31 December 2002 represented 32 days 2001 33 days of the average daily purchases for the year.
The trade creditors of Chelseld plc itself are not material in the context of the group.

18 Annual General Meeting Notice of the Annual General Meeting is set out on pages 78 and 79.
The meeting will consider as ordinary business the receiving of the report and accounts, the declaration of a final dividend, the election and re-election of directors, the approval of the  report and the re-appointment of the auditors.
The meeting will also consider as special business an authority to purchase the companys own ordinary shares, amounting to a maximum number of 28,184,528 ordinary shares, being 10 per cent of the issued ordinary share capital at 31 December 2002.
Any shares purchased in this way would be cancelled and the number of shares in issue would be reduced accordingly.
Any share purchases by the company will be made through the market and the resolution sets out the minimum and maximum prices at which they may be purchased.
The directors will continue to evaluate whether to exercise this authority in the light of prevailing market conditions.
The directors would only exercise this authority after considering the effects on earnings and net asset value per share and benets for shareholders generally.
Authorities to allot the companys remaining unissued ordinary shares and to allot ordinary shares otherwise than on a pro-rata basis are currently in existence and expire in April 2007.
