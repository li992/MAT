TULLOW OIL PLC annual report and accounts 2002 31 DIRECTORS REPORT The Directors submit their Report together with the audited consolidated financial statements for the year ended 31st December 2002.
1. Business Review and Future Developments The principal activities of the Company and its subsidiary undertakings the Group are oil and gas exploration and production and the provision of technical services to its joint venture partners.
Information on the Companys principal subsidiary undertakings is contained in Note 10 to the financial statements.
A full review of the Groups activities during the year, recent events and future developments is contained in the Chairmans Statement on pages 2 to 5, the Operational Review on pages 8 to 25 and the Financial Review on pages 26 to 28.
Results and Dividends The results of the Group for the year ended 31st December 2002 are set out in the Profit and Loss Account on page 40.
The profit on ordinary activities after taxation amounted to 12,795,601 2001 - 9,309,789.
The Company intends to propose and pay a dividend of 1p per ordinary share during 2003.
This is subject to the approval by the High Court of Ireland of an application to write off the accumulated deficit on the Profit and Loss Account of its Irish subsidiary against that Companys share premium account, the completion of the related accounting work and shareholder approval.
Post Balance Sheet Events Since the balance sheet date Tullow has continued to progress its exploration, development and business growth strategies.
On 10th March 2003, the Company announced the Placing of 17,900,000 new ordinary shares at a price of 80p.
The Company also announced that it had agreed the principal commercial terms of a banking facility of $100 million, which may be increased to $250 million in certain circumstances, to allow the Company to pursue its acquisition strategy.
On 11th March 2003, Tullow signed the Kiarsseny Marin production-sharing contract in Gabon.
This licence contains a number of existing oil discoveries.
On 2nd April 2003, Tullow announced the results of exploration wells in Algeria, India and the United Kingdom.
In each case the wells were unsuccessful and Tullow and its partners are evaluating the results in advance of determining future plans for the licences.

Supplier Payment Policy It is Company and Group policy to settle all debts with creditors on a timely basis and in accordance with the terms of credit agreed with each supplier.

Auditors During the year Arthur Andersen resigned as the Companys joint auditor following the agreement that it reached with Deloitte & Touche under which partners and staff from Arthur Andersen joined Deloitte & Touche.
The Directors used their powers under the Companies Act 1985 to appoint Deloitte & Touche as the Companys joint auditors with Robert J. Kidney & Co. A resolution to re-appoint the joint auditors and authorising the Directors to fix their remuneration will be submitted to the Annual General Meeting.
