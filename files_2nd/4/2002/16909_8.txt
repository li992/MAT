Directors Remuneration Report This report has been prepared to meet the requirements of the Directors Remuneration Report Regulations 2002 and will be put to shareholders as a separate ordinary resolution at the Annual General Meeting.
Information not subject to audit Remuneration Committee The principal function of the Boards Remuneration Committee is to determine Group policy on service agreements, remuneration, benefits and terms and conditions of employment of the Companys Executive Directors.
The Committee consisted of Mr J A Napier Chairman, Mr P A Barrett from 4 March 2002 to 18 June 2002, Mr J H Newman from 18 June 2002 and Mr S A Wauchope all of whom were or are Non-Executive Directors.
The Committee did not receive any material advice or services to assist on any matter during the year.
Policy on Directors Remuneration The Remuneration Committee seeks to structure remuneration packages on an individual basis appropriate to the level of responsibility, but generally designed to attract, retain and motivate high calibre individuals with a competitive package of basic salary, incentives and rewards.
Executive Directors The current remuneration package for Executive Directors consists of basic salary, bonus, pension benefits, share option schemes and other benefits including a company car and medical and permanent health insurance.
Only basic salary is pensionable.
Salaries for Executive Directors are reviewed annually by the Committee.
Salaries are increased each year by the retail price index at the date of review.
Incremental increases in salary are also given for additional responsibilities.
All Executive Directors are entitled to earn bonuses not exceeding 50% of their salary, based on profit targets and personal objectives.
No bonuses have been paid in respect of 2002.
Details of Directors emoluments are given on page 20.
Non-Executive Directors The fees payable to the Non-Executive Directors are set by the Remuneration Committee with reference to the Board.
Mr J H Newman will receive an annual fee of 90,000 for his role as Non-Executive Chairman.
Other Non-Executive Director fees were reviewed in May 2002 and increased to 25,000 per annum.
Details of fees paid to Non-Executive Directors are given on page 20.
The fees of Mr J A Napier and Mr P J Hudson are paid to Kelda Group plc.
