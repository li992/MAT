Pensions The company operates a number of pension schemes.
Certain eligible employees including T J Smith and M J Gilbert are members of the Express Ltd defined benefit scheme while other eligible employees including H Sondergaard are members of the Arla Foods Ltd defined benefit scheme.
Employees who have joined the company since a specified date 31 January 2002 for Express Ltd, 1 January 2002 for Arla Foods Ltd including N D Peet and S D Stevens have been eligible only to join a defined contribution or stakeholder plan.
Pensionable earnings are calculated on base salary only.
The amount of accrued pension for each director is set out on page 29, together with comparative amounts adjusted for inflation.
The company will introduce certain simplifications and other modifications to the pension schemes with effect from 6 April 2006.
In particular a pensionable salary cap based on the current earnings cap will be retained following the abolition of the earnings cap.
This will be adjusted annually in line with movements in the retail prices index.
Service contracts T J Smith entered into a new service contract with the company in July 2004.
This provides for one year's notice except that this rises to 18 months in the special case of termination by Arla Foods amba in the two years following October 2005.
On becoming Chief Executive T J Smith has agreed in principle to replace this with a service contract providing for 12 months' notice in all circumstances, subject to agreement between him, the company and Arla Foods amba.
Apart from T J Smith, all executive directors have 12-month notice periods.
The service contract of M J Gilbert expires on 1 June 2006.
As at 30 November 2005, the unexpired term of M J Gilberts contract was 182 days.
It is intended that N D Peet and S D Stevens will enter, in due course, into full service contracts providing for one years notice.
The companys policy, to the extent consistent with its contractual obligations, is to apply the principle of mitigation to any payments of compensation on termination.
The service contract of T J Smith provides that no mitigation shall be applied if he leaves the company as a result of termination of the contract.
Compensation is calculated by reference to salary and benefits payable over the notice period.
