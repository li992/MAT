Subsequent events In January 2010, the Group achieved a 525 million refinancing of Lakeside, Thurrock ahead of the CMBS maturity in mid 2011.
Also in January 2010 the Group entered into a conditional contract for disposal of The Westgate Centre, Oxford for gross proceeds of 56 million.
This is expected to complete during the second quarter of 2010.
On 26 February 2010 the Victoria Centre Partnership made an early repayment of 48 million on the bank loan due in 2013.
On 2 March 2010 the company acquired 100% of the issued share capital of Chelmsford Property Investments Limited from Capital & Counties Debenture PLC, a fellow subsidiary undertaking of Liberty International PLC, for consideration of 1.
On 25 March 2010 the company acquired 100% of the issued share capital of WRP Management Limited from Capital & Counties Debenture PLC, a fellow subsidiary undertaking of Liberty International PLC, for consideration of 9,868,387.
Since the year end the Group has triggered an asset-specific loan covenant breach.
The 31 December 2009 annual valuation of 52 million for the Xscape Braehead property, which is owned by the Xscape Braehead Partnership, a 50 per cent joint venture between Capital Shopping Centres and a subsidiary of Capital & Regional PLC, indicated a Loan to Value ratio in excess of that specified in the 49 million loan facility secured on the property.
Following submission of the valuation to the lender, they served a notice of breach on the Partnership, triggering the cure period.
Discussions between the lender and the Partnership as to potential solutions to the breach are well advanced.
The Groups immediate parent company, Liberty International PLC, announced on 9 March 2010 its intention to reorganise by way of a demerger into two distinct divisions, Capital Shopping Centres and Capital & Counties.
At the date of approval of these accounts, with shareholder approval received the demerger is subject only to Court approval.
Once received Liberty International PLC will change its name to Capital Shopping Centres Group PLC.
Contingent liabilities There were no contingent liabilities at 31 December 2009.
At 31 December 2008, the Group had contingent liabilities as shown.
The Groups joint venture with Land Securities, the St. Davids Limited Partnership, made annual rental payments of approximately 2.5 million per annum in respect of land to be used for car parking space.
If this arrangement had ceased, the partnership would have been liable to make a payment of approximately 58 million to compulsorily purchase the land, 50 per cent, which represents the Groups interest in the partnership, may have to be funded by the Group.
Ultimate parent company The immediate and ultimate parent company is Liberty International PLC, a company incorporated and registered in England and Wales, copies of whose consolidated financial statements may be obtained from the Company Secretary, 40 Broadway, London, SW1H 0BT.
