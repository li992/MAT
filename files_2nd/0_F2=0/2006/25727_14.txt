Directors Responsibilities in Respect of the Accounts Company law requires the Directors to prepare The Directors are responsible for ensuring that accounts for each financial year in accordance with proper accounting records are kept which disclose United Kingdom Generally Accepted Accounting with reasonable accuracy at any time the financial Practice which give a true and fair view of the state position of the Company and enable them to of affairs of the Company as at the end of the year ensure that the accounts comply with the and of the net return for the year.
They are also responsible for those accounts, the Directors are required to: safeguarding the assets of the Company and hence for taking reasonable steps for the prevention and select suitable accounting policies and then detection of fraud and other irregularities.
apply them consistently: make judgements and estimates that are reasonable and prudent: and state whether applicable accounting standards have been followed, subject to any material departures disclosed and explained in the accounts.
