Directors Responsibilities Statement The directors are required by the Companies Act 1985 to prepare financial statements which give a true and fair view of the state of affairs of the Company and the Group at the end of the financial year and of the profit for the year then ended.
In preparing the financial statements appropriate accounting policies have been used and applied consistently and reasonable and prudent judgements and estimates have been made.
The financial statements have been prepared in accordance with applicable accounting standards.
The directors are responsible for maintaining adequate accounting records which disclose at any time with reasonable accuracy the financial position of the Company.
The directors are also responsible for the system of internal control and for safeguarding the Companys assets by taking reasonable steps to ensure the prevention of fraud and other irregularities.
