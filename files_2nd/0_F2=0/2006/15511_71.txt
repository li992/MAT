Post-retirement benefits The G. E. C. 1972 Plan the UK Pension Plan is the principal pension fund of the telent Group and is a funded defined benefit plan.
A full valuation for accounting purposes was carried out as at 31 March 2005 by independent qualified actuaries under IAS 19, the results of which are disclosed in the notes to the consolidated accounts note 35.
A supplementary report has been produced by the actuaries to update the IAS 19 position to the FRS 17 position.
As the Company is unable to identify its share of the underlying assets and liabilities in the UK Pension Plan, the costs are recorded on a cash basis as permitted by FRS 17.
The pensions cost charge to the Company in respect of employees who are members of the UK Pension Plan consists of employers contributions payable.
The Company contributions to the UK Pension Plan in the year amounted to 7.3 million 2005: 0.3 million of which 7 million was a special voluntary contribution paid in March 2006 to reduce the plan deficit.
The balance was calculated as 8.2% 2005: 8.2% of members pensionable pay.
The major assumptions used by the actuaries to determine the liabilities on a FRS 17 basis for the significant defined benefit plans are the same as those disclosed in the consolidated accounts under the IAS 19 basis note 35.
