Treasury policies recommend covering up to six months in advance on forecast exposures using derivative products comprising of forward foreign currency contracts and currency options, although the Group has not used currency options in the last two years.
In all cases the implementation of these derivative instruments has been negotiated to match expected purchases and they therefore qualify for hedge accounting.
The fair value of cash ow hedges in place at 29 April 2006 is nil 2005: net 0.1m gain.
Other hedging activity At 1 May 2005, the Group had in place a number of foreign currency swaps in order to hedge against the provision of foreign currency loans made to the Groups overseas subsidiaries.
The fair value of these foreign exchange contracts at that time was a net 0.8m liability.
Since the introduction of the global cash pooling system in the period ended 29 April 2006, the Group has ceased to make loans that require to be hedged in this way using foreign currency contracts.
Thus there are no such contracts outstanding at 29 April 2006.
Based on its current debt levels, the Group moves to a net cash position during its third quarter and therefore interest rate exposure is limited.
Consequently, no interest rate hedges have been implemented.
Interest rate exposures continue to be monitored in accordance with the Groups treasury policies.
