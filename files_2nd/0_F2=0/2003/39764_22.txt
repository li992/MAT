notice of annual general meeting Notice is hereby given that the thirty-third Annual General Meeting of Scottish Radio Holdings plc will be held at the registered office, Clydebank Business Park, Clydebank, Glasgow G81 2RX on Monday 26 January 2004 at 11am for the following purposes: As ordinary business 1.
To receive and adopt the financial statements of the company for the year ended 30 September 2003 and the reports of the directors and auditors thereon.
To declare a final dividend in respect of the year ended 30 September 2003.
To approve the Remuneration report for the year ended 30 September 2003.
To re-elect as a director: Lord Gordon of Strathblane CBE Resolution 4 5.
To re-elect as a director: Mr Jamie Matheson Resolution 5 6.
To re-elect as a director: Mrs Sunny Crouch Resolution 6 7.
To re-appoint KPMG Audit Plc as auditors and to authorise the directors to agree their remuneration Resolution 7 As special business To consider and, if thought fit, pass the undernoted resolutions 8, 11 and 12 as ordinary resolutions and resolutions 9 and 10 as special resolutions: 8.
"That the Board be and it is hereby generally and unconditionally authorised, pursuant to Section 80 of the Companies Act 1985 ""the Act"", to exercise all the powers of the company to allot relevant securities within the meaning of Section 80 of the Act up to the aggregate nominal amount of the total unissued share capital of the company for the time being, provided that this authority shall expire on the earlier of the conclusion of the next following Annual General Meeting of the company after the passing of this resolution and 25 April 2005 save that the company shall be entitled to make at any time before the expiry of this authority any offer or agreement which would or might require relevant securities to be allotted after the expiry of this authority and the Board may allot relevant securities in pursuance of any such offer or agreement as if the authority conferred hereby had not expired."
That subject to the passing of Resolution 8, the Board be and it is hereby empowered pursuant to Section 95 of the Act to allot equity securities within the meaning of Section 94 of the Act for cash pursuant to the authority conferred upon it by Resolution 8 as if Section 89 1 of the Act did not apply to any such allotment, provided that this power shall be limited: i to the allotment of equity securities in connection with any rights issue in favour of the holders of Ordinary Shares of 10p each where the equity securities respectively attributable to the interests of all such holders of Ordinary Shares of 10p each are proportionate as nearly as may be to the respective numbers of Ordinary Shares of 10p each held by them provided that the Board may make such arrangements in respect of overseas holders of shares: fractional entitlements: or to resolve any other legal or practical problems arising in connection with any such rights issue, as the Board may consider necessary or convenient: and ii to the allotment otherwise than pursuant to sub-paragraph i of this resolution of equity securities up to an aggregate nominal amount of 166,892: and shall expire on the earlier of the conclusion of the next following Annual General Meeting of the company after the passing of this resolution and 25 April 2005, provided that the company may before such expiry make any offer or agreement which would or might require equity securities to be allotted after such expiry and the Board may allot equity securities in pursuance of any such offer or agreement as if the power conferred hereby had not expired.
That the company be and is hereby authorised to purchase for cancellation its own fully paid Ordinary Shares of 10p each by way of market purchases within the meaning of Section 163 3 of the Act upon and subject to the following conditions: i the maximum number of Ordinary Shares which may be purchased is 3,334,514 representing approximately 9.99% of the issued share capital as at 20 November 2003: ii the maximum price at which Ordinary Shares may be purchased is an amount equal to 105% of the average middle market quotation taken from The London Stock Exchange Daily Official List for the five business days immediately preceding the day on which the Ordinary Shares are contracted to be purchased and the minimum price is 10p per Ordinary Share, in both cases exclusive of expenses: and iii the authority to purchase conferred by this resolution shall expire at the earlier of the conclusion of the next Annual General Meeting of the company after the passing of this resolution and 25 April 2005, provided that any contract for the purchase of Ordinary Shares of 10p each as aforesaid which has been concluded before the expiry of such authority may be executed wholly or partly after such authority expires.
