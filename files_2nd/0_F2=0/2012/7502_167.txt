CAPITAL RISK MANAGEMENT The Company manages its capital to ensure that it is able to continue as a going concern while maximising the return to stakeholders through the appropriate balance of debt and equity.
The capital structure of the Company consists of debt, which includes the borrowings disclosed in note 49, cash and cash equivalents and equity comprising issued capital, reserves and retained earnings as disclosed in notes 52 and 55 and the Company Statement of Changes in Equity.

The main purpose of these financial instruments is to raise finance for the Companys operations.
The Company also has various other financial instruments such as trade receivables and trade payables which arise directly from its operations.
The main risks arising from the Companys financial instruments are interest rate risk, foreign currency risk, credit risk and liquidity risk.

The Company manages liquidity risk by maintaining adequate reserves and banking facilities and continuously monitoring forecast and actual cash flows.
Included in note 21 are details of the undrawn facilities that are available to the Company and the Group to further reduce liquidity risk.
With the exception of deferred and contingent consideration and the bank overdrafts and the revolving credit facilities, all of the Groups financial liabilities are due for payment within two years, based on contractual payment terms.
