Long Term Incentive Plan LTIP The LTIP is a discretionary plan for executive Directors and senior managers which replaced the Senior Management Equity Investment Plan SMEIP in 2007.
It comprises two elements: first it provides for the grant of matching awards based on the proportion of annual bonus applied by participants in the purchase of shares in the Company and held by the Employee Benefit Trust: and secondly it provides for the grant of performance awards.
Both awards, granted over shares in the Company, vest either in full or in part at the end of the three year performance period subject to meeting pre-defined targets.
In the year to 31 January 2011 participating employees applied a proportion of their annual cash bonuses for the year ended 31 January 2010 to purchase 103,112 288,730 shares of Alliance Trust PLC at a price of 3.48 2.84 per share.
Matching awards of up to 297,750 527,449 shares and performance awards of up to 657,194 650,544 were granted.
Matching awards and performance awards made during the year were valued at 485,000 900,000 and 1,072,000 1,110,000 respectively.
The fair value of awards granted during the year was calculated using a binomial methodology.
The assumptions used were a share price of 3.40 2.83, share price volatility of 21% 33.7% based on a long term average 5 year weekly average, dividend yield of 2.4% 2.8%, a risk free interest rate of 1.21% 3.77% and forfeiture of nil nil.
The cumulative charge to the income statement during the year for the cost of the LTIP 2010: LTIP & SMEIP awards referred to above was 1,294,000 878,000 for the Group and 845,000 893,000 for the Company.
Per IFRS 2 the costs of matching and performance awards for each plan are expensed over the three year performance period.
These costs are only adjusted if certain vesting conditions are not met, for example if a participant leaves before the end of the three year vesting period.
