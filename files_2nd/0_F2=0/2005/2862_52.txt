10697 Capita 2005 back 14 3 06 09:54 Page 82 82 Capita Notes to the consolidated financial statements 25 Financial instruments continued 2005 disclosures continued Hedges Fair value hedges The Group has in issue xed rate dollar and sterling bonds which it has hedged through a combination of interest rate and currency swaps.
At 31 December 2005 and 31 December 2004, the Group had an interest rate swap in place with a notional amount of 55.0m whereby it receives a xed rate of interest of 6.44% and pays a variable rate of 1% above LIBOR on this notional amount.
The swap is being used to hedge the exposure to changes in the fair value of its 55.0m of 6.44% bonds.
The secured loan and interest rate swap have the same critical terms.
Also, at December 2005 and December 2004, the Group had in place currency swaps with notional amounts of $66.0m and $36.0m whereby it receives a xed rate of interest of 6.1% and 6.47% respectively and pays a variable rate of 1% above LIBOR on these notional amounts.
The swap is being used to hedge the exposure to changes in the value of its US Dollar issued bonds.
There are no significant concentrations of credit risk within the Group.
The maximum credit risk exposure relating to financial assets is represented by the carrying value as at the balance sheet date.
