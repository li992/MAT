Critoph ACA Accounting policies and basis of preparation The accounts for the company have been prepared under UK GAAP, whilst the Group accounts have been prepared under International Financial Reporting Standards.
The company accounts have been prepared under the historical cost convention, in accordance with applicable accounting standards under UK GAAP and on a going-concern basis.
For accounting periods commencing after 1 January 2005 the company has adopted FRS 20 Share-based payment, FRS21 Events after the balance sheet date, FRS 22 Earnings per share, FRS 25 Financial instruments: disclosure and presentation and FRS 26 Financial instruments: measurement.
The primary impacts of these has been to include a charge in respect of share based payments and an adjustment to the timing of recognition of dividends proposed but not approved or paid.
As a result of these new accounting standards comparative information in respect of the year ended 31 December 2004 has been restated.
Net assets and reserves at 31 December 2004 are 7,561,000 higher than previously reported as the final dividend has not been accrued and the value of share options has been accounted for and net assets and reserves at 1 January 2006 are 8,187,000 higher than would have been reported before applying FRS 20 and FRS 21.
The share options have been accounted for as an expense in the company in which the employees are employed, using valuation based on the Stochastic simulation model.
In accordance with an available election in FRS 20, share based awards granted before 7 November 2002 have not been subject to a charge.
An increase in the investment held by The Restaurant Group plc in the subsidiary in which the employees are employed, with corresponding increase in equity, is recognised in the account of The Restaurant Group plc.
The value is accounted for as a capital contribution into relevant Group subsidiaries that employ the staff members to whom awards of share options have been made.
