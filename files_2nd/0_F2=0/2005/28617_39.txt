Debt securities in issue 2005 2004 000 000 Unsecured loan notes, repayable: 1 year or less but over 3 months 141 286 141 286 The fair value of debt securities in issue was not materially different to their carrying value.
The fair value is based on discounted cash flows using a current yield curve appropriate for the remaining term to maturity.
Debt securities in issue are unsecured loan notes that were issued as part of the consideration of a number of UK companies acquired in 2002.
The loan notes are repayable on the third or fourth anniversary of the date of issue but noteholders can require the Company to repay the principal sum or part thereof after six months and one day have expired from the date of issue, subject to 30 business days notice being given in writing.
Interest is payable at the Barclays bank base rate, in arrears on 30 June and 31 December.
Repayment will only be made on an interest payment date.
